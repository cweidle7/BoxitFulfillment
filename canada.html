UNDERSTANDING USMCA

What "duty-free" actually means

The USMCA (formerly NAFTA) lets goods that qualify under its rules of origin move between the US, Canada, and Mexico without customs duties. To qualify, a product generally must be made, or sufficiently transformed, within North America.

We determine whether each SKU qualifies, prepare the certificate of origin, and file it correctly. If a product doesn't qualify, we'll tell you the duty rate up front so there are no surprises.

FREQUENTLY ASKED

Shipping to Canada, answered

Will my customers get charged duties on delivery?+

No. With our DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) option, all duties and taxes are calculated and prepaid at checkout, so nothing is collected at the door.

How do I know if my product qualifies for USMCA?+

We review your SKUs against USMCA rules of origin and tell you which qualify for duty-free treatment, and the duty rate for any that don't.

How long does delivery to Canada take?+

Most major metros, including Toronto, Vancouver, and Montreal, see 2–5 business days once an order leaves our dock, with full carrier tracking.

Do you handle returns from Canadian customers?+

Yes. We provide Canadian return options and process returns back into your inventory, keeping cross-border logistics simple for your buyers.
